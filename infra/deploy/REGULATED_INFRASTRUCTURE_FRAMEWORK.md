> ## CORE PRINCIPLES FOR ALL REGULATED INDUSTRIES
> 
> | Principle | Implementation |
> |---|---|
> | **Truth is Not for Sale** | The system does not charge for truth, verification, or access to evidence. Monetization is for infrastructure, uptime, and support only. |
> | **Evidence Over Trust** | The system never assigns trust scores or reputation rankings. It only records evidence of contributions, compliance, and actions. Humans judge. The system records. |
> | **No Substitute Authority** | The system never decides, recommends, or nudges. It only surfaces options, models scenarios, and flags constraints. All decisions require explicit human approval. |
> | **No Optimization of Humans** | The system optimizes logistics, not people. It does not engage in preference mining, behavioral tuning, or persuasive design. |
> | **Data Sovereignty** | The user owns their data. The system provides tools to export, manage, and delete it. No data is used for training without explicit, revocable consent. |
> | **Explainability by Design** | Every action and piece of data is traceable to its origin. The system can always explain *why* it did something by pointing to the human decision or rule that triggered it. |
>

> ## ARCHITECTURAL MANDATES
> 
> 1.  **The Sanctuary Kernel is immutable.** It cannot be modified, only built upon.
> 2.  **All bolt-ons must assume the Sanctuary invariants.** They cannot question or bypass them.
> 3.  **The human/system boundary is non-negotiable.** The system is a tool, not a partner, peer, or authority.
> 4.  **Shutdown semantics are fail-closed.** In case of doubt or conflict, the system stops and awaits human instruction.
> 5.  **Memory is external evidence.** The system has no internal memory of past interactions, only pointers to an external, auditable record that the human controls.
